Hou Pressed

A cool small juice company that supports local farmers in Hawaiʻi.

The Challenge

This project asked to create a company which included a brand strategy that defined the project's objectives and encompassed the client's market positioning, target audience, brand personality, essential attributes, and potential customer touchpoints. The branding required a cohesive set of assets that collectively formed the brand identity along with labeling designs.

The Solution

In the brand strategy, I defined the brand voice, positioning statement, audience, personality, core attributes, and customer touchpoints. While word-mapping and sketching, I asked myself, “What things convey “local”, "fresh”, “trustworthy”, “friendly”, and “community-driven”? I selected the strongest concepts to guide the asset designs and explored various mediums and styles. After much experimentation, the watercolor style aligned with the voice of the brand. I created Hawaiian indigenous birds with bodies of local island fruits done with paint and Illustrator to embody a sense of warmth, locality, purity, and simplicity. I picked a color palette that reflected the vibrancy of the flora and fauna found on the islands.

BRAINSTORM PROCESS

I decided to utilize indigenous Hawaiian birds for the main focus of my branding. I experimented with different mediums but ultimately landed on a mix of watercolor and vector to create my birds.

ITERATIONS

Prior to finalizing my choice, I experimented with a range of label designs, exploring visual elements, typography, and layouts. This iterative process allowed for thorough exploration and refinement, leading to a well-informed decision on the final design.

PRINT PRODUCTION

The files were organized into layers to streamline the print production process. Ongoing communication with the print shop was equally as important, guaranteeing the file was configured accurately and met the necessary specifications for printing.
